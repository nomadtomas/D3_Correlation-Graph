Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Consistent association was derived between lower income and higher smoking, leading to the assumption that smoking is more prevelant amongst financially challenged individuals.

In the obesity perspective, it seems to show a significant correlation between higher income and lowered obesity. This pattern leads us to believe the higher income indiduals tend to spend more money on higher priced foods, which tend to be nonprocessed.

The impact financial stability has on healthy living seems to be quite significant. However, this could also be added to the notion that, higher income individuals also tend to have a higher education.
